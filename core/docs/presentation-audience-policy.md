# ProductOS Presentation Audience Policy

Purpose: Define how V4 presentation outputs should adapt by audience, stakes, and communication mode without letting narrative polish override truth.

## Rule

Presentation packaging should be chosen by audience need, not because slides are available.

Readable docs remain the default review surface.

Presentation output is justified when:

- leadership or executive review needs fast decision framing
- roadmap, release, or strategy communication needs a stronger narrative package
- a meeting, async deck, or one-page brief materially improves alignment speed

## Audience Modes

### Leadership

- lead with the answer or decision request
- keep risks, confidence, and unresolved questions visible
- prefer concise slides with explicit recommendation language

### Cross-Functional Working Review

- balance recommendation with operating detail
- show dependencies, owners, and decision impact clearly
- use more evidence density than leadership decks

### Working Team

- allow deeper detail and implementation context
- prioritize operational next steps over polished narrative
- do not suppress open questions for presentation neatness

### Customer

- require customer-safe redaction and claim review
- never include internal uncertainty language without deliberate customer framing
- treat customer-facing presentation as Tier 3 by default

## Mode Policy

### Async

- self-explanatory slides
- visible source labels
- enough context to survive without presenter narration

### Live

- stronger headline and cue structure
- allow some supporting detail to stay in notes
- do not move critical risk or blocker information entirely off-slide

### Memo And One-Page

- keep one message per section
- preserve evidence trace in the appendix or footnotes
- avoid decorative layouts that reduce skim speed

## Safety Rules

- slides are renderings, not the source of truth
- recommendation strength must stay proportional to evidence strength
- any audience-safe filtering must be explicit
- when in doubt, choose the stricter validation tier
