# Strategic Discovery Model

Purpose: Define how ProductOS upgrades discovery from artifact production into asymmetric strategic insight generation.

## Core idea

Discovery should not begin only when the PM has a clear research question. ProductOS should help the PM detect weak signals, classify uncertainty, generate materially different options, and pressure-test strategic narratives before commitment.

## Strategic layers

- strategic sensing: detect weak signals and cluster them into hypothesis candidates
- uncertainty management: classify unknowns by type, impact, and cheapest next learning method
- option generation: force multiple distinct paths instead of polishing the default frame
- defensibility framing: capture why now, why us, and what becomes hard to copy
- premortem learning: identify failure causes, early warning indicators, and reversal triggers before commitment
- strategic memory: preserve prior theses, later outcomes, and repeated traps

## Operating rules

- no major strategy recommendation should rely on a single frame when multiple plausible paths exist
- no weak-signal pattern should become a commitment without traceable supporting evidence
- no important unknown should remain untyped when a cheaper learning method is available
- no major bet should advance without a premortem and a narrative stress test

## Intended PM advantage

The goal is not merely faster research. The goal is earlier detection, better framing, stronger option quality, and compounding strategic judgment over time.
