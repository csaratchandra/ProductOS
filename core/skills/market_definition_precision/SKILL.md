# Market Definition Precision

## Purpose

Define the market around the actual workflow, job, and buying context instead of loose category language.

## Trigger / When To Use

Use when category labels feel broad, ambiguous, or detached from how the buyer experiences the problem.

## Inputs

- product wedge
- target segment and persona context
- workflow and decision context
- competitor and customer evidence

## Outputs

- precise category definition
- target market scope statement
- explicit category tradeoffs and exclusions

## Guardrails

- do not hide ambiguity behind trendy category labels
- do not define the market so broadly that the wedge disappears
- do not define the market so narrowly that adjacent expansion becomes incoherent

## Execution Pattern

- identify the job, workflow, and buyer context
- define the narrow category that makes the wedge intelligible
- note adjacent categories and why they are not the primary frame
- preserve the market definition through downstream strategy artifacts

## Validation Expectations

- the market definition should be specific enough to constrain posture and competition
- the target scope should align with the chosen wedge and persona
- category exclusions should be explicit when they matter to positioning
