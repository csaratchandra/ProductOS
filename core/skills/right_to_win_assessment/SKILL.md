# Right To Win Assessment

## Purpose

Separate structural advantage from internal optimism when ProductOS claims why the product can win.

## Trigger / When To Use

Use when a strategy packet includes a right-to-win statement, differentiated angle, or advantage claim.

## Inputs

- product capabilities and constraints
- competitor comparison
- customer and market evidence
- current proof and distribution posture

## Outputs

- right-to-win assessment
- weak or unsupported advantage claims
- revised advantage framing with confidence

## Guardrails

- do not mistake internal familiarity with the product for external advantage
- do not claim distribution, proof, or switching leverage that does not exist
- do not hide thin advantage behind polished language

## Execution Pattern

- list the implied advantage claims
- test each claim against market, product, and proof reality
- separate real structural advantage from aspiration
- rewrite the right-to-win statement to match the evidence bar

## Validation Expectations

- each advantage claim should map to something concrete and defensible
- unsupported claims should be downgraded or removed
- the final right-to-win should survive competitor and proof scrutiny
