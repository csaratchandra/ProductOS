# Concept Risk Surfacing

## Purpose

Expose the risks that make a concept fragile before it becomes a PRD or delivery commitment.

## Trigger / When To Use

Use when a `concept_brief` exists but the wedge, assumptions, or downstream handoff risks are still too implicit.

## Inputs

- draft `concept_brief`
- linked problem framing
- strategy and posture context

## Outputs

- risk summary for the concept
- clarified wedge and boundary language
- recommendation to proceed, revise, or park

## Guardrails

- do not restate assumptions as if they were mitigated
- keep wedge dilution and scope-expansion risks explicit
- preserve the current claim boundary

## Execution Pattern

1. inspect the wedge and positioning for overreach
2. list what could invalidate the concept quickly
3. translate risks into handoff notes for PRD and story work
4. recommend whether the concept is decision-ready or still review-needed

## Validation Expectations

- risk items should be concrete and downstream-relevant
- the concept boundary should remain clear after revision
- reviewers should understand what still needs confirmation
