# PM Coaching And Growth Workflow

Purpose: Turn repeated workflow and artifact patterns into actionable PM coaching outputs that improve capability over time without bypassing PM judgment.

## Inputs

- workflow history across weekly, monthly, and quarterly reviews
- recent artifacts and rubric results
- repeated-pattern observations
- prior coaching outputs and commitment follow-through
- curriculum tracks and mastery rubric guidance

## Outputs

- coaching summary
- skills-gap profile
- PM growth review
- repeated-pattern report
- next-practice recommendations with follow-up measures

## Cadence

- weekly: tactical coaching summary focused on current operating habits and immediate corrections
- monthly: skills-gap profile grounded in repeated evidence and benchmarked against the mastery rubric
- quarterly: PM growth review covering trend direction, preserved strengths, and stagnation risk

## Operating Sequence

1. Collect the evidence window and cluster signals by recurring strong and weak patterns.
2. Map each pattern to a mastery dimension and curriculum track.
3. Separate urgent operational corrections from slower developmental coaching.
4. Compare current evidence to prior review periods to determine improvement, stagnation, or regression.
5. Recommend a small set of next practices with explicit follow-up metrics and review dates.

## Rules

- coaching must be grounded in observable artifact or workflow patterns
- distinguish urgent operational correction from developmental coaching
- record both recurring weak patterns and recurring strong patterns
- show evidence of improvement or stagnation across repeated tasks
- preserve PM control over final judgment and commitment decisions
- do not recommend more priority focus areas than the evidence can justify
- each coaching action should name the behavior to change, the context where it appears, and the signal that will show improvement
- repeated issues without improvement should escalate to a higher-intensity coaching path rather than being restated verbatim
