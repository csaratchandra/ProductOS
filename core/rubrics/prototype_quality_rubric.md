# Prototype Quality Rubric

Purpose: Evaluate whether a ProductOS prototype is merely illustrative, internally usable, or strong enough to behave like a credible v1 candidate.

Use this rubric during `ux_design_review` and whenever a feature score depends materially on prototype quality.

## Dimensions

- interaction clarity: the core flow is understandable without narration and the next action is obvious
- workflow credibility: the prototype behaves like a plausible real workflow instead of a staged demo
- information hierarchy: the most important state, evidence, and decisions are visually prioritized
- trust and traceability: the prototype shows what is proven, what is inferred, and what still requires approval
- visual intent: the surface feels deliberate and product-shaped rather than generic or placeholder-heavy
- accessibility resilience: status, structure, and flow remain understandable without relying only on color, motion, or dense visuals

## Ratings

- `excellent`: decision-ready, distinctive, trustworthy, and strong enough to serve as the current internal reference bar
- `acceptable`: useful and credible, but still missing enough polish, hierarchy, or interaction sharpness that it should not anchor superpower claims
- `weak`: communicates the concept, but the flow, trust model, or visual structure is still too rough for serious PM use
- `fail`: misleading, decorative, or too incomplete to support real workflow learning

## Promotion Bar

- do not call a prototype "world-class" unless it earns `excellent`
- do not treat a prototype as a v1 candidate if workflow credibility or trust and traceability is below `excellent`
- any `acceptable`, `weak`, or `fail` result must produce concrete improvement actions
- if the prototype is strong enough for PRD advancement but not `excellent`, say so explicitly instead of overstating product quality
