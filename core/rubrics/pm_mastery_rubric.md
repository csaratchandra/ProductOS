# PM Mastery Rubric

Purpose: Evaluate whether ProductOS outputs and coaching loops are improving PM judgment, communication quality, execution discipline, and evidence-backed decision-making over time.

## Dimensions

- judgment quality: recommendations reflect tradeoffs, sequencing logic, and explicit decision criteria
- evidence discipline: claims trace back to source artifacts, current data, or stated assumptions
- communication effectiveness: updates, reviews, and plans are concise, audience-fit, and action-oriented
- execution reliability: work is tracked clearly, owners are explicit, and blocked states are surfaced early
- learning velocity: repeated mistakes decline and coaching converts into observable improvement
- systems thinking: the PM accounts for dependencies, portfolio impact, and risk propagation

## Ratings

- `excellent`: the PM shows compounding improvement, strong judgment, and reliable evidence-backed execution
- `acceptable`: the PM is operationally effective, with visible strengths and a few bounded growth gaps
- `weak`: the PM can produce outputs, but recurring judgment, traceability, or execution gaps remain
- `fail`: the PM repeatedly misses evidence, ownership, risk, or decision-quality bars in ways that block trust

## Review Cadence Guidance

- weekly: inspect coaching uptake and repeated workflow issues
- monthly: review strengths, gaps, and artifact-quality trends
- quarterly: assess whether capability growth is compounding or stagnating

## Minimum Growth Bar

- repeated weak patterns are identified with evidence
- at least one concrete next practice is assigned
- progress is compared against prior periods
- strong patterns are preserved, not only weak patterns corrected
