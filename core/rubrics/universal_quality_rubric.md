# Universal Quality Rubric

Purpose: Provide a default quality bar for ProductOS artifacts and workflows.

## Scoring

Use `strong`, `acceptable`, or `weak` for each dimension.

## Dimensions

### 1. Clarity

- `strong`: the artifact is easy to understand and does not hide its main point
- `acceptable`: mostly clear, with minor ambiguity
- `weak`: hard to parse, vague, or overloaded

### 2. Traceability

- `strong`: claims and recommendations are clearly linked to entities, evidence, or upstream artifacts
- `acceptable`: most important claims are traceable, but some links are thin
- `weak`: important claims are unsupported or cannot be traced

### 3. Structural Integrity

- `strong`: the artifact follows its schema or template cleanly
- `acceptable`: minor structural gaps exist but do not block use
- `weak`: missing required structure or inconsistent fields make the artifact unreliable

### 4. Decision Usefulness

- `strong`: supports a clear next step or decision
- `acceptable`: somewhat useful but leaves obvious questions unresolved
- `weak`: creates noise without helping the PM decide or act

### 5. Scope Discipline

- `strong`: stays within validated scope and does not invent unsupported detail
- `acceptable`: small unsupported inferences exist but are visible
- `weak`: introduces scope or conclusions not supported by upstream inputs
